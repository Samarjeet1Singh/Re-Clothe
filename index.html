Our Process

From Nature to Fabric

We use natural dyes made from fallen flowers and leaves to create eco-friendly textiles.

Step 1: Collecting

We gather fallen marigolds, hibiscus, and other natural materials.

Step 2: Extracting

These plants are boiled to extract natural pigments.

Step 3: Dyeing

The extracted dyes are used to color organic fabrics like silk and cotton.

Step 4: Weaving

Skilled artisans weave and finish the fabric into unique, eco-friendly textiles.

From Old Clothes to New Fashion

Upcycling old garments into modern, stylish pieces with sustainability at the core.

Sorting & Cleaning

Old clothes are sorted and washed to prepare for transformation.

Deconstructing & Designing

Fabric pieces are repurposed, and new designs are planned.

Cutting & Sewing

The upcycled fabrics are cut and sewn into stylish, fresh garments.

Finishing & Branding

Final touches are added before the garments are ready for sale.

About Us

Our Story

Re-Clothe was born from a passion for sustainability and ethical fashion. Our goal is to redefine clothing production by using natural dyes and upcycled materials, creating fashion that is both beautiful and eco-friendly.

Our Mission & Vision

We are committed to promoting ethical fashion by ensuring transparency in production. Our vision is to create a sustainable world where fashion and the environment go hand in hand.

Circular Economy Model

At Re-Clothe, we embrace a circular economy by recycling old fabrics into new designs. Our approach minimizes waste, reduces carbon footprints, and supports a greener planet.
